AntiVir: A Database for Antiviral Plants

AntiVir is a manually curated database of antiviral plants that compiles comprehensive information on plant species, their taxonomic families, associated phytochemicals, and virus-specific antiviral activity. The database integrates experimentally reported antiviral evidence to provide structured, searchable, and statistically summarized data for plant-based antiviral research.

AntiVir is a curated database compiling experimentally reported antiviral activity of medicinal plants. The database integrates plant taxonomy, virus classification, type of extract, active compounds, and bibliographic references to support research in plant sciences, virology, and drug discovery.

Classification

  • Plant groups: Angiosperms, Gymnosperms, Thallophyta
  • Virus genome: DNA / RNA
  • Virus classes: Class I – VII
  • Virus families: Herpesviridae, Flaviviridae, Picornaviridae, Hepadnaviridae, Orthomyxoviridae, etc.

Plant Families

The database includes antiviral plants from diverse families such as:

  • Asteraceae
  • Fabaceae
  • Meliaceae
  • Lamiaceae
  • Rubiaceae
  • Apocynaceae
  • Polygonaceae
  • Malvaceae
